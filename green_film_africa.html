Who We Are

Africa's First Regenerative Film Organisation

Green Film Africa is a pan-African sustainable filmmaking initiative operating under the parent company Brooks Media Ltd. We are a team of experts dedicated to creating viable networks and solutions for a sustainable creative industry in Africa.

Our strategic and systemic solutions are tailored to production companies, broadcasters, service providers, associations, and institutions. We specialise in the exchange and expansion of know-how relevant to the creative industries, consultancy and project management for green film production, CO₂ expertise and resource management, and training in sustainable project management.

We believe the future of the African film industry lies in finding eco-friendly solutions that benefit both the environment and the stakeholders. Our goal is to connect you to green film production experts on the continent, ensuring that your production process is environmentally friendly and regenerative — not just sustainable.

Our Framework

The 5 Rs of Regenerative Filmmaking

Beyond reduce-reuse-recycle. A complete system upgrade for how African productions relate to the world they film in.

R

Rethink

Challenge every assumption from the script stage. Does this scene need a flight? Does this set need new lumber? Design the problem out before cameras roll.

R

Redesign

Redesign your production system. Circular props, local crew, solar power, zero-waste catering. The best sustainability decision is one you never have to reverse.

R

Reduce

Measure your carbon. Use a CO₂ calculator. Track fuel, flights, food, and waste every day. What gets measured gets managed.

R

Reuse

Rent wardrobes, share props between productions, keep mitumba over fast fashion. One production's waste is another's resource. That's PropBank™.

R

Regenerate

Leave the location better than you found it. Plant trees. Train locals. Partner with the community. Give back more than you took. This is the goal.

Industry Standards

ESG — The Framework That Controls
Who Gets to Film in Africa

Netflix, BBC, and global brands now require ESG compliance from every production company they hire. Understanding this framework is a career necessity.

E

Environmental

How a production manages its impact on the natural world

  • Carbon emissions and energy use (Scopes 1, 2 & 3)
  • Waste generation and disposal methods
  • Water consumption and pollution
  • Impact on biodiversity and ecosystems
  • Use of hazardous materials on set
  • Diesel generator usage — Africa's #1 culprit
S

Social

How a production manages relationships with people

  • Fair wages and safe working conditions for all crew
  • Diversity, equity, and inclusion in hiring
  • Community engagement and benefit-sharing
  • Respect for Indigenous land rights at locations
  • Mental health and crew welfare on set
  • Hiring local crew over flying in foreigners
G

Governance

How a production makes decisions — its accountability structure

  • Transparent financial reporting and anti-corruption
  • Clear written sustainability commitments and policies
  • Named accountable Green Consultant on every production
  • Compliance with environmental and labour law
  • Ethical supply chain and vendor screening
  • Green Production Plan signed before Day 1

New Roles. Real Jobs.

This Is a Career Conversation, Not Just an Ethics One

Green production is creating an entirely new category of film industry roles. Green Film Africa trains for these and helps connect talent to opportunity.

Green Producer

Manages sustainability from script to screen. Runs carbon audits, builds ESG reports, ensures Albert and Green the Bid compliance. One of the fastest-growing roles in global production.

Eco Steward / Green PA

On-set sustainability coordinator. Tracks waste, manages catering compliance, trains crew. Entry level with huge growth potential across Africa and internationally.

Carbon Analyst

Calculates production footprints using tools like Albert. Produces data for ESG disclosures and offset planning. High demand from broadcasters and brands.

Climate Storyteller

Director, writer, or content creator focused on climate. The IPCC explicitly identifies media as a key lever for climate action. This is a funded, growing space.

Regenerative Location Scout

Identifies and manages locations with community and ecological protocols. Ensures post-production restoration. A growing role in East Africa as international shoots increase.

Green Tech Supplier

Supplies solar rigs, LED grids, water stations, and EV transport to productions. A B2B business opportunity — companies like Kenya Grip & Sparks and Roam are already building this market.

Frequently Asked Questions

Your Questions, Answered

What is Green Film Production?

Green Film Production is an environmentally friendly and sustainable way of producing films. It involves eco-friendly practices that minimise the negative impact of film production on the environment — covering everything from energy use and waste management to community engagement and supply chain choices.

What is the difference between sustainable and regenerative filmmaking?

Sustainability asks "how do we stop the damage?" — it aims for net neutral impact. Regeneration asks "how do we reverse it?" — it aims for net positive impact, leaving the location, community, and ecosystem better than before. One is a ceiling. The other is a direction.

Does going green cost more?

No — going green saves 15–20% on average production costs. Fuel, waste disposal, and logistics are all cheaper when you plan them sustainably. Beyond savings, ESG compliance is now required by Netflix, BBC, and major brands. Without it, you cannot access those commissions.

Who are your services designed for?

We work with production companies, broadcasters, service providers, associations, film schools, and institutions. Our programmes serve everyone from secondary school students to senior producers — because the shift to regenerative filmmaking requires the whole ecosystem to move together.

What is PropBank™?

PropBank™ is Africa's first circular film resource database — a platform where productions can list, search, rent, swap, or donate props, wardrobe, lighting, camera gear, sound equipment, art department materials, vehicles, catering equipment, and post-production technology. One production's wrap is another's prep.
